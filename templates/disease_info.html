Skin Diseases Information

Melanocytic Nevi (NV)

Description: Common moles, usually benign growths of melanocytes.

Symptoms: Small, dark brown spots or growths; usually round or oval; may be flat or raised.

Melanoma (MEL)

Description: A dangerous form of skin cancer that develops in melanocytes.

Symptoms: Irregular borders, multiple colors, rapidly changing size or shape, bleeding or itching.

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

Description: The most common type of skin cancer, often appearing as a raised bump.

Symptoms: Shiny or pearly bump, open sore that doesn’t heal, reddish patch, or scar-like area.

Benign Keratosis-like Lesions (BKL)

Description: Non-cancerous growths that mimic malignant skin conditions.

Symptoms: Waxy, scaly, or wart-like growths; usually brown, black, or light tan; often appear “stuck on.”

Actinic Keratoses (AKIEC)

Description: Pre-cancerous spots caused by sun exposure, may develop into carcinoma.

Symptoms: Rough, scaly patches on sun-exposed areas; may be pink, red, or brown; can feel dry or itchy.

Dermatofibroma (DF)

Description: Harmless skin nodules, often firm and small.

Symptoms: Firm, raised nodule (often on legs); brown, red, or purple; may be itchy or tender when pressed.

Vascular Lesions (VASC)

Description: Includes hemangiomas and pyogenic granulomas.

Symptoms: Red, purple, or blue lesions; may bleed easily; can appear as small bumps, spots, or clusters of blood vessels.
